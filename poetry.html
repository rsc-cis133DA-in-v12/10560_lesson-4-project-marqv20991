And That Language

By Lawrence Joseph

And that language that parts the future from the past the poet slips into,

two people the same pulse the sounds around the poem-was it September?-

saxophone and mandolin sounds on the radio,

a grammar of trees in the wind in the leaves in ecstasies of grace,

this company of love—you, at first sight; you-a language,

the softest touch a language; beauty’s eyes, your eyes,

unclose me anywhere I am.
